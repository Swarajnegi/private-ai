## 3. The 5 Cost Layers — Indian Investing in US Equity

For every ₹1L round-tripped via INDmoney into US stocks:

| Layer | Rate (2026) | Type | Lever |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TCS on remittance (LRS)** | 20% above ₹10L/FY | Refundable (cash-flow drag) | Stay under ₹10L/FY — trivially achieved at current SIP size |
| **INDmoney forex markup** | ~0.5% in + ~0.5% out | Permanent | Switch broker or use FoF (FoF route blocked, see §4) |
| **Bank forex spread** | 0.5–1.2% on wire | Permanent | Bank choice |
| **US dividend WHT** | 25% after W-8BEN | Creditable via Form 67 + Schedule TR | Low-yield ETFs reduce surface |
| **India LTCG on sale (≥24 mo)** | 12.5% flat | Permanent | Already optimal at 10–20yr horizon |

**Compliance overhead:** Schedule FA every year (mandatory; ₹10L penalty under Black Money Act for non-disclosure). India receives INDmoney holdings via FATCA — non-disclosure is high-detection.

**Total drag:** ~14-15% of *gains* (12.5% LTCG + ~2-3% lifetime forex round-trip). **Taxes don't eat profits — they take a fixed proportional bite.**

**Verdict:** INDmoney direct US route is now *more reliable for execution* than India-domiciled FoFs despite higher friction. Forex + Schedule FA is a known cost; FoF route has hidden tail-risk in subscription rejection + premium volatility.

## 5.6 Concentrated-Book Guardrails (the discipline that makes v3.1 survivable)

A concentrated book is defensible at 23 with a 30-yr horizon + family backstop — but ONLY with the discipline professional concentrated investors use. The bet is high-beta-to-one-factor by choice; these rules keep it a *disciplined* bet, not a YOLO. Review quarterly.

**2. Sell/trim discipline (you currently have none — concentrated investors who survive all do).**
- **MRVL:** joins S&P 500 June 22, 2026 after a +57% month → classic "sell-the-news." Consider trimming into that strength.
- **DRAM ETF:** bought at +150% YTD at cycle euphoria; Micron's own CFO warns of 20-40% memory price collapse 2027-28. Trim 30-40% before Q4-2026 as market reprices 2028 supply.

**3. Entry staggering.** Never lump-buy at local highs / +100%+ YTD. DCA the ₹17K/mo in; don't chase a green candle.

**4. The kill-variable watch.** The ONE thing that ends this thesis is **AI-capex moderation.** Watch quarterly: hyperscaler capex guidance (already capping below expectations June 2026), NVDA ASP (−18% recently), memory margins (Samsung 42%→35%). Don't sell on noise — but know exactly what you're tracking. June-2026 macro read = **mid-cycle inflection**, not early-cycle. Position accordingly: hold, don't aggressively add at the top.

**5. Drawdown doctrine — "only up" is FALSE and deleted.** Every great compounder falls 50%+ repeatedly: NVDA −66% in 2022 (and 4 separate >50% drawdowns historically); Cisco (the most valuable company on Earth in 2000, real revenue + backlog) is *still* below its 2000 peak 26 years later. The investors you follow survived 2022 precisely because they expected the −66% and held. Believing "only up" is what makes weak hands panic-sell at the bottom — the single most expensive mistake available.
