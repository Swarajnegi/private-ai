# Netweb Technologies India Ltd (NETWEB)  

**Financial performance:** Netweb is a high-end computing (HPC/AI) systems maker. In FY2025 (Mar’25) it delivered **₹11,490.2 M** operating income (+58.7% YoY), with EBITDA ₹1,600.1 M (13.9% margin) and PAT ₹1,144.8 M (+50.8% YoY)【8†L674-L681】.  By FY2026 (Mar’26) revenue surged to **₹21,836 M** (+90% YoY), with operating EBITDA ₹2,148 M and PAT ₹2,058 M (PAT margin ~9.4%)【26†L179-L184】.  (Netweb ended FY26 with net cash ~₹833 M and zero net debt【26†L179-L184】.) Key growth came from **AI/space-defense** and **HPC** segments – the AI GPU systems segment grew ~460% YoY and contributed ~43% of FY26 sales【26†L106-L114】, while the high-performance computing (HPC/supercomputer) segment grew ~50% YoY to ~₹4,027 M in FY25【6†L25-L29】【26†L106-L114】.  Private-cloud servers and enterprise storage also saw strong double-digit gains. 

**Analyst forecasts (1Y/3Y/5Y):** After the FY26 results, analysts remain very bullish. Consensus for FY2027 (Mar’27) projects **revenues ≈₹41.6–42.0 B** (increasing ~90–100% from FY26) and EPS ~₹66–68【29†L52-L58】.  That implies roughly doubling of FY26 sales by FY27 (∼93% annualized growth through 2027【29†L74-L82】).  Price targets are rich: the current 1-year consensus target is ~₹4,305 (range ₹4,110–4,500)【21†L107-L113】 – about 15–20% above recent levels. (For context, 3 analysts rate Netweb a “Strong Buy” and give an average 12‑month target ~₹4,059【9†L617-L625】.)  Our view is similarly bullish: if Netweb sustains even ~35–40% CAGR, revenues could well double again over 3–5 years.  For example, a simple ~40% annual growth from FY26’s ₹21.8 B base yields ∼₹60 B by FY29 and ~₹140 B by FY31.  (This is an illustrative upside scenario, reflecting the company’s under-penetrated market and heavy orderbook; actual outcomes will depend on execution and market conditions.)  

**Technology & market role:** Netweb is **India’s only full-stack domestic supplier** of high-end computing (HPC) and AI server systems【26†L139-L144】.  Its core business units – **HPC/supercomputers, Private Cloud servers, and AI/GPU systems** – are directly tied to national technology drives (Make-in-India, PM Gati Shakti, National Supercomputing Mission).  For example, Netweb last year commissioned India’s first end-to-end HPC manufacturing plant and launched *“Skylus.ai”*, a plug‑and‑play GPU‑infrastructure platform【8†L651-L659】.  It partners with NVIDIA (Blackwell GB200 GPUs) and AMD (new Genoa CPUs) to enable sovereign AI compute.  Its **AI segment** (mainly custom GPU workstations and servers) grew 460% in FY26【26†L106-L114】.  The management highlights that AI/space‑defense customers now account for ~14–15% of revenue【6†L0-L3】, underscoring how “AI infrastructure” is a rapidly growing vertical.  Netweb’s technology edge lies in **in-house design and manufacturing** (90+ patents/trademarks, PLI scheme) of servers/storage/switches. In short, Netweb is strategically positioned as India’s HPC/AI infrastructure “powerhouse,” enabling data centers, research labs, and defense networks with indigenously designed supercomputers and GPU farms【8†L651-L659】【26†L139-L144】.  

# Sterlite Technologies Ltd (STL)  

**Financial performance:** STL is a global optical networks and fiber‑based telecom hardware company. In FY2025 (Mar’25) it reported **₹3,996 Cr** consolidated revenue (slightly down from ₹4,083 Cr in FY24) with EBITDA ₹452 Cr (11.3% margin)【16†L48-L52】.  Q4 FY25 alone saw **₹1,052 Cr** revenue (+25% YoY) and EBITDA ₹146 Cr (13.9% margin)【16†L48-L52】.  The optical-networking business (cables, fibers, connectivity) was the bulk, while the digital/data-center solutions segment (Neuralis AI-DC) is smaller but growing.  (STL ended FY25 with a strong order book: MD Ankit Agarwal reports orders up ~110% and sees a “multi-year demand cycle”【35†L459-L468】【35†L463-L471】.)  

**Analyst forecasts (1Y/3Y/5Y):** Post-FY25, analysts have raised their outlook. Consensus for FY2027 projects **revenues ≈₹62.3 B** (i.e. ~₹6,230 Cr)【18†L52-L59】, about 30% CAGR over FY25.  EPS is forecast to jump accordingly.  The consensus 12‑month price target has climbed – Simply Wall St notes analysts lifted it ~84% to **₹423**【18†L61-L64】, while TradingView shows ~**₹433** (range ₹405–454)【20†L94-L100】.  (Current stock ~₹230.) These targets imply ~80%+ upside.  In our view, STL is riding structural secular trends (5G, FTTH, AI data centers): if its ~30% growth outlook holds, revenues could roughly **double** by FY30.  For example, data-center and FTTH expansion could drive STL’s top line toward ₹10,000 Cr+ in 3–5 years.  Beyond 5 years, adoption of advanced optics (hollow‑core, multicore fiber) in AI/5G networks could sustain growth, though exact forecasts are uncertain.  

**Technology & market role:** Sterlite is a **fiber‑optics and connectivity leader** targeting cutting‑edge network needs. Its optical cables and fibers form the “backbone” of next‑gen AI and telecom networks【33†L110-L119】. STL actively markets **AI‑optimized data-center solutions** (“AI‑DC” portfolio) – high‑density fiber and switching kits built for GPU‑rich clusters【30†L48-L53】. It runs end-to-end R&D (glass preform → fiber → cable) and holds ~800+ patents.  Key technology initiatives include **hollow‑core fiber** (for ultra‑low-latency AI/finance networks【32†L106-L114】【32†L178-L186】) and **multicore fibers** (“MCF”) for high bandwidth.  Management notes that upcoming **GPU‑dominated data centers** will drive 30–35% of global fiber demand【35†L463-L471】, and STL’s products (like 6,912‑fiber cables) are explicitly designed for this surge【33†L158-L163】.  Additionally, STL serves 5G and rural broadband rollouts (FTTH), security sensors, and cloud-network architectures with its integrated portfolio【35†L450-L459】【35†L463-L471】.  In short, Sterlite embeds itself at the core of “bleeding‑edge” networks – from hyperscale AI data centers to next‑gen 5G transport – by innovating at the material (fiber), device (optical modules), and system (network services) levels【35†L498-L502】【32†L178-L186】.  

**Summary of near-term/medium-term outlook:** Both companies are riding strong tailwinds. Netweb’s management targets ~35–40% revenue growth in FY27 (order book already ≈ last year’s revenue)【23†L30-L36】; analysts assume even higher (~90% per annum to FY27【29†L52-L58】). Sterlite’s book and market trends imply continued double‑digit growth (US/India data-center and FTTH spending), with guidance of ~30%+ (an expanding global market)【35†L463-L471】.  Price-wise, fair‑value models and brokerage consensus imply significant upside over 1–2 years for both (NTWEB in ₹4k+ range【21†L107-L113】, STL in ₹4–5k range【18†L61-L64】【20†L94-L100】). Over 3–5 years, if execution matches opportunity, these firms could see revenues multiply, driven by sustained AI and digital infrastructure builds. However, investors should note that such forecasts embed optimism – risks include execution slippage or market slowdowns – but current data suggest both Netweb and Sterlite are well‑placed for the next wave of “AI + connectivity” growth【26†L139-L144】【35†L463-L471】.
