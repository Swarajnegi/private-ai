## 3. The 5 Cost Layers — Indian Investing in US Equity

For every ₹1L round-tripped via INDmoney into US stocks:

| Layer | Rate (2026) | Type | Lever |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TCS on remittance (LRS)** | 20% above ₹10L/FY | Refundable (cash-flow drag) | Stay under ₹10L/FY — trivially achieved at current SIP size |
| **INDmoney forex markup** | ~0.5% in + ~0.5% out | Permanent | Switch broker or use FoF (FoF route blocked, see §4) |
| **Bank forex spread** | 0.5–1.2% on wire | Permanent | Bank choice |
| **US dividend WHT** | 25% after W-8BEN | Creditable via Form 67 + Schedule TR | Low-yield ETFs reduce surface |
| **India LTCG on sale (≥24 mo)** | 12.5% flat | Permanent | Already optimal at 10–20yr horizon |

**Compliance overhead:** Schedule FA every year (mandatory; ₹10L penalty under Black Money Act for non-disclosure). India receives INDmoney holdings via FATCA — non-disclosure is high-detection.

**Total drag:** ~14-15% of *gains* (12.5% LTCG + ~2-3% lifetime forex round-trip). **Taxes don't eat profits — they take a fixed proportional bite.**

## 4. Why Direct US Route via INDmoney Over India-Domiciled FoFs (2026 Reality)

In theory the cleanest route to US index exposure is an India-domiciled FoF (Motilal Oswal Nasdaq 100 FoF, Mirae NYSE FANG+ ETF, ICICI Pru Nasdaq 100): zero LRS, zero TCS, zero Schedule FA. In practice that route has a structural problem in 2026:

**SEBI caps:**
- $7 billion industry-wide for overseas securities + funds
- $1 billion per AMC sub-limit
- $1 billion industry-wide ETF cap (separate)

**Current state (May 2026):** Only ~28 international MFs and 6 international ETFs are open for fresh investments. The rest are intermittently closed. Feb 2, 2026 saw another industry-wide suspension event.

**Consequences for the two largest US-tracking funds:**
- **Motilal Oswal Nasdaq 100 FoF** (₹7,691 Cr AUM): close to MOSL's $1B AMC sub-limit. Open/closed in cycles. SIP mandates often get rejected.
- **Mirae Asset NYSE FANG+ ETF**: creation/redemption gated → trades at persistent **5–15% NAV premium** on NSE. Buying at premium means you start at a loss.

**Verdict:** INDmoney direct US route is now *more reliable for execution* than India-domiciled FoFs despite higher friction. Forex + Schedule FA is a known cost; FoF route has hidden tail-risk in subscription rejection + premium volatility.

**Primary pick:** QQQM (0.15% TER, Nasdaq 100 — 101 holdings, ~60% tech weight, top-10 = ~50%). Selected for higher trailing 5-yr returns (~18%/yr vs VUG ~15.5%/yr) driven by mega-cap tech leadership and AI capex cycle. Deployed as lump-sum STP from incentive windfalls + RIL exit proceeds, not monthly SIP — monthly slot reserved for single-stock AVGO conviction. AVGO is ~5% of QQQM — overlap with direct AVGO position accepted as deliberate AI-infra conviction. **Known gap accepted:** QQQM's Nasdaq-only filter structurally excludes NYSE-listed AI second-derivative names (ANET, ORCL, LLY). Plan: plug via direct single-stock positions in Phase 2 once monthly capacity scales — ANET (AI networking), ORCL (cloud AI), as ₹2-3K/mo each. ## 8. Discipline Notes (the things that actually determine outcomes)

1. **Variance is the cost of equity returns, not a defect.** A 40–60% drawdown will happen. Don't panic-sell. Don't stop SIPs.
2. **Temperament beats strategy.** The split architecture protects against both panic-selling on drawdowns AND doubling-down at peaks. Both are temperament failures, not strategy failures.
3. **"Max profits in 1-2 years" is the wrong framing.** Pushes toward concentration, single-stock bets, market timing — three habits that wreck long-term wealth. Refocus on "compound reliably for 20 years."
4. **Sequence-of-returns risk is real near goals, not near start.** At 23 with 30-year horizon, you WANT crashes — they're when SIPs accumulate cheap units. At 50 with 5-year horizon, crashes destroy.
5. **Family backstop is an asset, but plan to pay it forward.** Father at 52 earning ₹3-3.5L/mo is the implicit guarantor of aggressive equity tilt. By age 33 the user should be in position to be father's anchor as he approaches 65, not just inherit one.
6. **Step-up requires salary growth.** 12% annual step-up on SIPs assumes salary roughly triples in 10 years and 9x in 20 years. Plausible for competent Data Engineer transitioning into senior roles, but not automatic. Step-up should be reviewed annually against actual salary growth.
7. **Calibrated upside expectations:**
   - 10 yr (₹27.5K/mo + step-up): ~₹79L corpus base case
   - 20 yr: ~₹4.7Cr corpus base case
   - 30 yr: ~₹19-22Cr corpus base case
   - These assume returns hold up (India 14% INR, US 12% USD). Real outcomes will vary by 30-50% in either direction.
