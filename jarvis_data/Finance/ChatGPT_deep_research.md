# Top US Stock Picks from Q1 2026 13F Filings

Analysis of the latest (Q1 2026) 13F filings reveals a strong hedge‐fund tilt toward AI and tech leaders.  For example, Ray Dalio’s Bridgewater Associates **added** chip/AI names like Marvell (MRVL), Nvidia (NVDA) and Amazon (AMZN)【39†L175-L177】.  RenTec (Renaissance Technologies) **initiated** a new Apple (AAPL) stake, **boosted** its Nvidia holding and **sold** Amazon【34†L172-L174】.  Bill Ackman’s Pershing Square dramatically **cut** Alphabet (Google) exposure while **building** a large Microsoft (MSFT) position【36†L686-L690】.  Cathie Wood’s ARK funds list **Tesla (TSLA)** and **AMD (AMD)** as top holdings and added **Broadcom (AVGO)** in Q1【8†L270-L274】.  Warren Buffett’s Berkshire quietly **bought** Alphabet in Q1【8†L243-L247】.  In short, virtually every major investor favored large-cap tech/AI names.  

From this analysis, the following **10 US stocks** stand out as strong picks:

- **Amazon.com (AMZN):** A dominant e-commerce/cloud player.  Bridgewater in Q1 specifically *added* Amazon【39†L175-L177】. (Ackman’s Pershing had bought Amazon in prior quarters.)  Amazon’s AWS business and AI services are key growth drivers.  
- **Alphabet (Google, GOOGL):** Buffett’s Berkshire *picked up* Alphabet shares in Q1【8†L243-L247】.  Google’s AI leadership and ad-business make it a core holding for many funds.  
- **Nvidia (NVDA):** The leading AI chipmaker.  Both Bridgewater【39†L175-L177】 and Renaissance【34†L172-L174】 increased NVDA exposure.  Nvidia appears on almost every tech-growth watchlist.  
- **Apple (AAPL):** The iPhone and services giant.  Renaissance initiated a *new* multi-million-share Apple position【34†L172-L174】, signaling bullishness on its ecosystem and AI roadmap.  
- **Microsoft (MSFT):** The cloud/AI software leader.  Pershing Square disclosed a *major new* Microsoft stake (billions of dollars) in Q1【36†L686-L690】, citing AI and Azure as catalysts.  
- **Broadcom (AVGO):** A diversified semiconductor firm.  ARK Invest’s Q1 filings show a significant new Broadcom position【8†L272-L274】.  Broadcom benefits from data-center and networking growth.  
- **Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM):** The world’s largest contract chipmaker.  It’s held by multiple top funds (Tepper, Loeb, Coleman, etc.) as a proxy for AI-driven chip demand【17†L157-L159】. (TSM is traded via its US ADR.)  
- **Tesla (TSLA):** EV and AI/autonomy pioneer.  ARK lists Tesla as its **#1 holding**【8†L270-L272】.  Institutional interest in Tesla reflects its leadership in EVs and neural-net software.  
- **Marvell Technology (MRVL):** A fabless chipmaker for data centers.  Bridgewater *added* Marvell in Q1【39†L175-L177】, likely betting on its AI/data-center chips.  
- **AMD (AMD):** A top GPU/CPU maker.  ARK lists AMD as its **#2 holding**【8†L270-L272】.  After last year’s run, many investors still see AMD as a core AI/graphics play.

Each of these picks is backed by recent 13F moves by major managers【39†L175-L177】【36†L686-L690】【34†L172-L174】【8†L243-L247】. In other words, hedge funds and gurus are heavily overweighting them.
